Proposed Question

Are there differences in tap water characteristics across regions in South Australia?

This analysis explores whether tap water characteristics, including mineral composition and water treatment indicators, vary across regions in South Australia.

Proposed Data

South Australia was selected as the case study because, as one of Australia’s driest states, drinking water management is important across different regions. This raises the question of whether people living in different parts of South Australia experience measurable differences in tap water characteristics. The dataset was obtained from the Government of South Australia and contains 4,872 observations and 11 variables related to drinking water quality. Key variables include Region Name, System Name, Parameter, Average Value, Health Guideline, Aesthetic Guideline, and Disinfection. According to the metadata, average values are calculated as rolling values over the previous 12 months, making the dataset suitable for comparing regional differences in tap water characteristics.

Analysis 2: Identify which water quality parameters vary the most

To compare variation across water quality parameters, the coefficient of variation (CV) was calculated for each parameter across operational regions. CV standardises variation relative to the mean, allowing parameters measured on different scales and units to be compared more fairly.

# calculate variation by parameter
variation_summary <- water_quality_selected |>
  group_by(parameter) |>
  summarise(
    mean_value = mean(average_value, na.rm = TRUE),
    sd_value = sd(average_value, na.rm = TRUE),
    cv = sd_value / mean_value
  ) |>
  arrange(desc(cv))

Analysis 3: Explore variation between water supply systems within the Remote Communities region

While Analysis 1 compared average water characteristics between operational regions, water quality may still vary between individual water supply systems within the same region. Since system_name represents individual water supply systems, this analysis focuses on the Remote Communities region to explore local variation in water quality characteristics. Remote Communities was selected because remote areas may rely on different local water sources and supply systems, which could lead to greater variation in tap water characteristics. Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) was selected because it showed clear regional variation in earlier analyses and represents dissolved mineral concentration in tap water.

# filter Remote Communities TDS data
remote_tds <- water_quality_selected |>
  filter(
    region_name == "Remote Communities",
    parameter == "Total dissolved solids"
  )

# plot variation across systems
ggplot(remote_tds,
       aes(x = reorder(system_name, average_value),
           y = average_value)) +
  geom_point(
    color = "steelblue",
    size = 2
  ) +
  coord_flip() +
  labs(
    x = "Water Supply System",
    y = "TDS (mg/L)"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

From Figure 5,TDS levels varied substantially between water supply systems within the Remote Communities region, ranging from below 50 mg/L to around 800 mg/L. This suggests that local water sources and supply conditions may differ considerably even within the same operational region.
